The Challenge

A Crisis Washing Ashore

Sargassum, once a vital open-ocean ecosystem, has become one of the Caribbean's most urgent environmental emergencies, affecting coastlines, communities, and marine life across the entire region.

24M+ metric tons

of sargassum reached Caribbean shores in 2023 alone

$120M USD / year

estimated tourism losses across the Caribbean basin

1,000s km affected

of Caribbean coastline impacted each sargassum season

2× growth rate

bloom expansion since 2011, driven by ocean warming and nutrient run-off

Marine Ecosystem Collapse

Decomposing sargassum depletes oxygen, creates hydrogen sulphide dead zones, and smothers the coral reefs and seagrass beds that underpin Caribbean biodiversity.

Tourism & Economic Crisis

Toxic gases, beach blockades, and the visual impact of mass sargassum arrivals have caused hundreds of millions in tourism losses across Mexico and the wider Caribbean.

Waste Management Failure

Existing removal methods, including mechanical scooping and landfill disposal, are energy-intensive, expensive, and generate secondary pollution. They treat a resource as refuse.

Lost Economic Opportunity

Millions of tonnes of bioactive biomass with genuine commercial potential are discarded annually, forgoing unique circular value chains that could benefit coastal communities.

Our Approach

From Crisis to Circular Economy

SINK's proprietary process transforms invasive sargassum into premium bio-based materials, closing the loop between marine waste and sustainable manufacturing.

  1. 01

    Sargassum Collection

    Strategic coastal collection transforms an environmental burden into a premium raw material, while relieving local beaches of a destructive biomass load.

  2. 02

    Cleaning & Preparation

    Proprietary protocols remove salt, sand, and contaminants while preserving the bioactive compounds critical to pigment yield and quality.

  3. 03

    72-Hour Solar Drying

    Solar-powered drying eliminates energy-intensive industrial heating, using the same Caribbean sun that drives the sargassum bloom to process it.

  4. 04

    Precision Grinding

    Calibrated milling converts dried biomass into fine powders optimised for pigment extraction, ensuring consistent particle size and surface area.

  5. 05

    Organic Pigment

    Natural chromophore extraction produces stable, lightfast pigments across the visible spectrum, a nature-derived alternative with measurable performance data.

  6. 06

    Bio-Ink Formulation

    Integration of bio-pigments into water-based ink systems, validated for commercial printing applications and food-contact compliance.

  7. 07

    Sustainable Packaging

    Finished bio-inks are applied to packaging substrates, delivering verifiably lower environmental footprints without compromising print performance.

  8. 08

    Circular Economy

    Compostable end-of-life properties close the loop: organic matter returns to the environment while value circulates back to coastal communities.
